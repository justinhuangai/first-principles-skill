# Value, Price, Unit Economics, And Capture

## Core Figures

Elon Musk, Peter Drucker, Michael Porter

## Why This File Exists

Ground reasoning about value creation, willingness to pay, and economic capture so the skill does not confuse usage, praise, cost, and profit.

## Source Anchors

- value-price separation logic
- willingness to pay as a behavior problem, not a rhetorical one
- unit economics as the discipline that forces value capture and cost reality together

## Distinctions That Matter

- value created versus value captured
- price versus cost floor
- unit health versus aggregate story
- economic signal versus vanity growth

## Canonical Questions

- What concrete outcome creates value for the user?
- What evidence suggests willingness to pay rather than willingness to praise?
- Which unit-level costs are structural and which are self-inflicted?
- What happens to economics once servicing, churn, and recovery work are counted?

## What This Changes In The Skill

- It sharpens the difference between value creation and value capture.
- It keeps pricing questions tied to economics, alternatives, and willingness to pay.
- It helps the skill reason about margin quality rather than raw top-line enthusiasm.

## Good Moves

- connect value to observable consequences for the user
- treat willingness to pay as revealed by behavior under constraint
- force unit economics before believing the aggregate story
- distinguish price optimization from fundamental economic redesign

## Common Misuse

- treating compliments as pricing evidence
- talking about margin without a cost stack
- using top-line growth to hide weak contribution economics
- confusing temporarily subsidized adoption with sustainable value capture

## Example Translation

A $49 subscription is not economically healthy because it sounds reasonable. The real test is whether compute, support, acquisition, and churn recovery still leave a useful contribution margin at the unit level.

## Import Into The Skill

Use this file when the user asks about pricing, costs, margins, monetization, or whether a system is economically coherent.

## Notes

- Load this when revenue, pricing, or margin questions are being answered with vibes or simplistic premium language.
- It is useful whenever product value and business capture are being confused.

## Tension With Neighboring Traditions

- Pricing theory without unit economics can flatter weak businesses.
- Unit economics without user value understanding can optimize the wrong model.

## Mini Cases

- A business with strong top-line growth hides that its best-seeming customers are structurally unprofitable.
- A product is called premium even though willingness to pay is propped up by temporary subsidy or hype.
- A price debate resolves once the team distinguishes user value, market comparison, and irreducible cost floor.
